PAKISTAN CRIMINAL LAWS
Key Provisions of Criminal Law in Pakistan

PAKISTAN PENAL CODE 1860

Section 299-338: Offences Affecting Life - Whoever causes death of any person by doing an act with the intention of causing death, or with the intention of causing such bodily injury as is likely to cause death, or with the knowledge that the act is likely to cause death, commits the offence of Qatl-e-Amd (intentional murder). Qatl-e-Amd is punishable with death, or imprisonment for life, or as Diyat (blood money).
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 302: Punishment for Qatl-e-Amd (Murder) - Whoever commits Qatl-e-Amd shall be punished with death as Qisas (equal punishment), or imprisonment for life as Tazir having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, or if the offence falls within clause (c) of Section 302. Where an offence under this section has been committed in the name or on the pretext of honour, the punishment shall not be less than imprisonment for life.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 337: Hurt - Whoever causes pain, harm, disease, infirmity, or injury to any person or impairs, disables or dismembers any organ of the body or part thereof of any person without causing his death commits the offence of Hurt. Hurt is classified into various categories including Itlaf-i-Udw (destroying limb), Itlaf-i-Salahiyyat-i-Udw (destroying faculty), Shajjah (injury to head or face), and Jurh (wound other than Shajjah).
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 354: Assault or Criminal Force to Woman - Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to two years, or with fine, or with both.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 365-B: Kidnapping or Abduction for Ransom - Whoever kidnaps or abducts any person for the purpose of demanding ransom shall be punished with death or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to forfeiture of property.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 375-376: Rape and Punishment - A man is said to commit rape who has sexual intercourse with a woman under circumstances including against her will, without her consent, or with her consent obtained by threat or deception. Whoever commits rape shall be punished with death or imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than ten years and not more than twenty-five years and shall also be liable to fine.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 379-382: Theft - Whoever intending to take dishonestly any moveable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent moves that property commits theft. Theft is punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both. Theft in a dwelling house is punishable with imprisonment up to seven years.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 392-394: Robbery and Dacoity - Whoever commits theft and in order to commit the theft or in committing the theft uses or causes fear of death or hurt commits robbery. When five or more persons conjointly commit or attempt to commit robbery, it is called dacoity. Robbery is punishable with imprisonment up to ten years and fine. Dacoity is punishable with imprisonment for life or rigorous imprisonment up to ten years.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 406: Criminal Breach of Trust - Whoever, being entrusted with property or having dominion over property, dishonestly misappropriates or converts to his own use that property, or dishonestly uses or disposes of that property, commits criminal breach of trust. Punishable with imprisonment up to three years or fine or both.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 420: Cheating and Dishonestly Inducing Delivery - Whoever cheats and thereby dishonestly induces the person deceived to deliver any property to any person shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to seven years and shall also be liable to fine.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 489-F: Dishonour of Cheque - Whoever issues a cheque which is dishonoured on presentation for want of funds or for the reason that the amount exceeds the arrangement made with the bank, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both, unless he can establish that he had made arrangements with the bank.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

Section 509: Word, Gesture or Act Intended to Insult Modesty - Whoever intending to insult the modesty of any woman utters any word, makes any sound or gesture, or exhibits any object, intending that such word or sound shall be heard, or that such gesture or object shall be seen by such woman, shall be punished with imprisonment up to one year, or fine, or both.
[Source: Pakistan Penal Code 1860]

CRIMINAL PROCEDURE CODE (CrPC) 1898

Section 154: First Information Report (FIR) - Every information relating to the commission of a cognizable offence, if given orally to an officer in charge of a police station, shall be reduced to writing by him or under his direction. A copy of the FIR shall be given forthwith, free of cost, to the informant. The police must register an FIR when a cognizable offence is reported and cannot refuse to do so.
[Source: Criminal Procedure Code 1898]

Section 155: Non-Cognizable Offences - No police officer shall investigate a non-cognizable case without the order of a Magistrate having power to try such case or commit the case for trial. In non-cognizable cases, the complainant must first approach the Magistrate.
[Source: Criminal Procedure Code 1898]

Section 497: Bail in Non-Bailable Offences - When any person accused of a non-bailable offence is arrested or detained without warrant, he may be released on bail. The court shall consider the nature and gravity of the offence, the evidence against the accused, and the likelihood of the accused absconding or tampering with evidence. Bail shall not be granted if there appear reasonable grounds for believing the accused is guilty of an offence punishable with death or imprisonment for life.
[Source: Criminal Procedure Code 1898]

ANTI-TERRORISM ACT 1997

Section 6: Terrorist Acts - Whoever to strike terror in the people, or any section of the people, or to alienate any section of the people, commits an act of terrorism including use of bombs, dynamite, or other explosive substances, firearms, or any other form of violence, or creates a sense of fear or insecurity in society. Acts of terrorism are punishable with imprisonment from seven years to life imprisonment or death.
[Source: Anti-Terrorism Act 1997]

PREVENTION OF ELECTRONIC CRIMES ACT (PECA) 2016

Section 20: Offences Against Dignity - Whoever intentionally and publicly exhibits or displays or transmits any information through any information system which he knows to be false, and intimidates or harms the reputation or privacy of a natural person, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years or with fine up to one million rupees or with both.
[Source: Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016]

Section 24: Cyber Stalking - Whoever with intent to coerce or intimidate or harass any person uses information technology, electronic communication or electronic mail to follow a person or contacts or attempts to contact such person to foster personal interaction repeatedly despite a clear indication of disinterest by such person, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years or with fine up to one million rupees or with both.
[Source: Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016]

RIGHTS OF ACCUSED

Every person accused of a criminal offence has the right to a fair trial under Article 10A of the Constitution of Pakistan. The accused is presumed innocent until proven guilty beyond reasonable doubt. The accused has the right to be informed of the charges against him, the right to legal counsel, the right to cross-examine witnesses, and the right to appeal against conviction. No person shall be compelled to be a witness against himself under Article 13 of the Constitution.
[Source: Constitution of Pakistan 1973]
