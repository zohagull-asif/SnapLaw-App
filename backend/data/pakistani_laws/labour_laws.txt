PAKISTAN LABOUR LAWS
Compilation of Key Employment and Labour Provisions

FACTORIES ACT 1934 - WORKING HOURS AND CONDITIONS

Section 34: Weekly Hours - No adult worker shall be required or allowed to work in a factory for more than forty-eight hours in any week.

Section 35: Daily Hours - No adult worker shall be required or allowed to work in a factory for more than nine hours in any day. The periods of work of an adult worker in a factory shall be so fixed that no period shall exceed five hours and that no worker shall work for more than five hours before he has had an interval for rest of at least one hour.

Section 36: Spread Over - The periods of work of an adult worker in a factory shall be so arranged that inclusive of his intervals for rest they shall not spread over more than ten and a half hours in any day.

Section 37: Overtime - Where a worker works in a factory for more than nine hours in any day or for more than forty-eight hours in any week, he shall, in respect of overtime work, be entitled to twice his ordinary rate of wages. A worker shall not be required to work overtime for more than twelve hours per week.

Section 38: Weekly Holiday - No adult worker shall be required or allowed to work in a factory on a Sunday, and no deduction shall be made from his wages on account of his having been prevented from working on any Sunday.

INDUSTRIAL AND COMMERCIAL EMPLOYMENT (STANDING ORDERS) ORDINANCE 1968

Section 10: Termination of Employment - No employer shall terminate the employment of a permanent workman unless one month's notice in writing or wages in lieu thereof is given to the workman. The employer shall not terminate without just cause including misconduct, physical or mental incapacity, or redundancy.

Section 11: Dismissal - A workman guilty of misconduct may be dismissed without notice or wages in lieu thereof, but only after a proper inquiry in which the workman has been given an opportunity to explain his conduct.

Section 12: Gratuity - Every employer shall pay gratuity to every worker on termination of employment after not less than one year of continuous service at the rate of thirty days wages for each completed year of service.

MINIMUM WAGES ORDINANCE 1961

Section 3: The Government may fix minimum rates of wages for different classes of workers. No employer shall pay wages at a rate less than the minimum rate of wages notified by the Government.

Section 5: Any agreement to work for wages below the minimum wage is void. Minimum wage in Pakistan is periodically revised by the Federal and Provincial Governments.

PAYMENT OF WAGES ACT 1936

Section 3: Every employer shall be responsible for the payment to persons employed by him of all wages required to be paid. Wages shall be paid before the expiry of the seventh day after the last day of the wage period.

Section 7: Deductions from Wages - Deductions from wages may only be made for: fines imposed according to rules, absence from duty, damage or loss of goods expressly entrusted to the worker, housing accommodation provided by the employer, recovery of advances, income tax, and contributions to provident fund.

EMPLOYEES OLD-AGE BENEFITS INSTITUTION (EOBI) ACT 1976

Section 7: Every employer shall register with EOBI and contribute at the prescribed rate for each insurable employee.

Section 8: The employer's contribution shall be five percent of the minimum wage for each employee.

PROVINCIAL EMPLOYEES SOCIAL SECURITY ORDINANCE 1965

Section 23: Every employer shall register with the relevant Provincial Employees Social Security Institution and make contributions for employees earning below the prescribed wage ceiling.

PROTECTION AGAINST HARASSMENT OF WOMEN AT THE WORKPLACE ACT 2010

Section 2: Harassment includes any unwelcome sexual advance, request for sexual favors, or other verbal or written communication or physical conduct of a sexual nature, or stalking at the workplace.

Section 3: Every organization shall constitute an Inquiry Committee with three members, of whom at least one shall be a woman, to inquire into complaints of harassment.

Section 4: Every organization shall display a Code of Conduct at a conspicuous place in the workplace and adopt an anti-harassment policy.

WORKMEN'S COMPENSATION ACT 1923

Section 3: If personal injury is caused to a worker by accident arising out of and in the course of his employment, his employer shall be liable to pay compensation in accordance with the provisions of this Act.

Section 4: Compensation shall be payable regardless of any fault on the part of the worker, unless the injury was caused by the worker being under the influence of drink or drugs, or willful disobedience of safety rules.
